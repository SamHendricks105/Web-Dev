Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

The Salmon River in Idaho is a significant and iconic river known for its scenic beauty, challenging rapids, and rich history. The main fork of the Salmon River is often referred to as the "Main Salmon River" and is one of the longest free-flowing rivers in the contiguous United States. The Salmon River has historical significance, particularly in relation to the Lewis and Clark Expedition. The Corps of Discovery, led by Lewis and Clark, crossed the continental divide near the Salmon River headwaters in the early 19th century.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Desolation and Gray Canyons, located in Utah, are part of the Green River corridor and offer stunning landscapes, recreational opportunities, and a rich geological and cultural history. Desolation and Gray Canyons offer a chance to explore the beauty and solitude of Utah's wilderness while enjoying a variety of outdoor adventures. Whether you're seeking thrilling river trips or peaceful hikes through stunning landscapes, these canyons have something to offer for outdoor enthusiasts of all interests and experience levels.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

Rafting in the Grand Canyon, located in Arizona, is an iconic adventure that offers unparalleled scenery, thrilling whitewater rapids, and a deep connection to one of the world's most awe-inspiring natural wonders. Rafting in the Grand Canyon is an adventure of a lifetime, offering an immersive and unforgettable journey through one of nature's most spectacular landscapes. Whether you're seeking adrenaline-pumping rapids or peaceful moments of reflection amidst stunning scenery, a rafting trip in the Grand Canyon promises to be an extraordinary experience.
